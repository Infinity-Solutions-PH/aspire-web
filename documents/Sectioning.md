System Architecture: Tech-Voc Enrollment & Sectioning1. Core System Parameters & RolesAuthorized Role: Guidance Account. Only this role can create sections, set capacities, trigger batch automation, and execute manual overrides.Global Default Capacity: A dynamic integer (e.g., 40) set by Guidance. This serves strictly as the automated threshold, telling the system when to stop assigning students during a batch run.Student Attributes Required: Enrollment_Status (Unassigned, Sectioned, Pending_Track), GWA/Grade_Average, Gender, and Enrollment_Timestamp (for tie-breaking).Section Attributes Required: Section_Name, Is_Star_Section (Boolean), Current_Headcount, Assigned_Track (for Grade 8+).2. Core Algorithmic Rules (The Engine)When the system automates sectioning, it must strictly obey these foundational rules:Timestamp Tie-Breaker: If two students have the exact same grade and are competing for a final slot (especially in the Star Section), the system prioritizes the student with the older Enrollment_Timestamp (first come, first served).Gender-Balanced Serpentine Distribution: To ensure regular sections have equal academic baselines and gender ratios, the system splits unassigned students into male and female pools. It distributes the females using a serpentine pattern (Section 1 -> 5, then 5 -> 1), and then immediately follows with the males using the same snake pattern.The "Hard Stop" for Automation: If the batch algorithm detects that all available regular sections have hit the Global_Default_Capacity, it immediately halts. Any remaining students stay in the "Unassigned" pool.The Master Manual Override: The Guidance Counselor can manually assign any student to any section at any time. This action bypasses the Global Default Capacity. If Guidance assigns a student to a full Star Section, the headcount simply updates to 41/40 without requiring the global limit to be changed.3. The Sectioning WorkflowsA. Grade 7 / General Batch SectioningThis is the primary workflow for incoming students who do not yet have a specialized track.Step 1: Preparation: Guidance creates the sections (e.g., 1 Star Section, 4 Regular) and sets the Global_Default_Capacity to 40.Step 2: Trigger & Sort: Guidance clicks "Run Auto-Sectioning". The system gathers all Unassigned students and sorts them by GWA (Highest to Lowest).Step 3: Star Allocation: The system skims the top $N$ students (where $N$ = capacity) and assigns them to the Star Section. (If the Star Section is already full from a previous run, it skips this step).Step 4: Regular Allocation: The system takes the remaining pool, applies the Gender-Balanced Serpentine Distribution, and fills the regular sections.Step 5: Halt & Report: Once all regular sections hit 40, the system stops and reports: "Batch complete. X students assigned. Y students remaining."B. Handling Late Enrollees & The "Star Qualifier"Late enrollees bypass the batch process and drop into the "Unassigned" list.The UI Alert: Every time Guidance opens the Unassigned list, the system calculates the real-time average GWA of the current Star Section. If an unassigned late enrollee's grade is greater than or equal to that average, a visual badge ("⭐ Qualified for Star Section") appears next to their name.Action: Guidance sees the badge and can execute a Manual Override to place them in the Star Section, or assign them to the regular section with the lowest headcount.C. Grade 8: Tech-Voc Specialization RoutingBecause Grade 8 requires shop capacity management, auto-sectioning is delayed until track selection is finalized.Step 1: Student Selection: During enrollment, the student selects their desired specialization via their portal (e.g., ICT, Automotive). Their status becomes Pending_Track_Approval.Step 2: Guidance Approval: Guidance reviews the applications (potentially aided by system flags checking their Grade 7 exploratory grades) and approves the track.Step 3: Track-Based Batching: Approved students are moved to track-specific unassigned pools (e.g., Unassigned_ICT). Guidance then runs the standard Batch Sectioning algorithm (Detailed in A) specifically for those track pools.D. Senior High (Grade 11): The Dual-Pathway EnrollmentTo maintain clean database records without duplicate LRNs, Senior High enrollment splits into two distinct flows on the landing page:External Transferees: Clicks "New Applicant", fills out a blank form, uploads credentials, selects a TVL strand, and generates a new student_id.Internal Promoted Students: Clicks "Continuing Student", logs in with their Grade 10 credentials. The system migrates their demographic data and LRN into a new Grade 11 application, silently linking their old academic record for Guidance review. The student simply updates outdated info, picks their TVL strand, and submits.
